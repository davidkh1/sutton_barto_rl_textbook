\section*{Problem}

Suppose $\gamma = 0.9$ and the reward sequence is $R_1 = 2$ followed by an
infinite sequence of 7s. What are $G_1$ and $G_0$?

\section*{Solution}

\subsection*{$G_1$}

From time $t = 1$, all future rewards are 7:
\[
    G_1 = R_2 + \gamma R_3 + \gamma^2 R_4 + \cdots
    = 7 + 0.9 \cdot 7 + 0.9^2 \cdot 7 + \cdots
    = 7 \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \gamma^k
    = \frac{7}{1 - \gamma}
    = \frac{7}{1 - 0.9}
    = \boxed{70}
\]

using equation~(3.10): $\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \gamma^k = \frac{1}{1-\gamma}$.

\subsection*{$G_0$}

Using the recursive relation $G_0 = R_1 + \gamma\, G_1$:
\[
    G_0 = 2 + 0.9 \cdot 70 = 2 + 63 = \boxed{65}
\]
