\section*{Problem}

If the current state is $S_t$, and actions are selected according to a stochastic
policy $\pi$, then what is the expectation of $R_{t+1}$ in terms of the policy
function $\pi$ and the four-argument dynamics function $p$ (3.2)?

\section*{Solution}

The four-argument dynamics function is defined in equation~(3.2) as:
\[
    p(s', r \mid s, a) \doteq \Pr\{S_{t+1} = s', R_{t+1} = r \mid S_t = s, A_t = a\}
\]

To find $\mathbb{E}_\pi[R_{t+1} \mid S_t = s]$, we marginalize over actions
(weighted by the policy), next states, and rewards:

\begin{align*}
    \mathbb{E}_\pi[R_{t+1} \mid S_t = s]
    &= \sum_{a} \pi(a \mid s) \; \mathbb{E}[R_{t+1} \mid S_t = s, A_t = a] \\[8pt]
    &= \sum_{a} \pi(a \mid s) \sum_{s'} \sum_{r} r \; p(s', r \mid s, a)
\end{align*}

where the sums range over all $a \in \mathcal{A}(s)$, $s' \in \mathcal{S}^+$,
and $r \in \mathcal{R}$.

\subsection*{Derivation}

By the law of total expectation, conditioning on the action:
\[
    \mathbb{E}_\pi[R_{t+1} \mid S_t = s]
    = \sum_{a} \pi(a \mid s) \; \mathbb{E}[R_{t+1} \mid S_t = s, A_t = a]
\]

The inner expectation is computed from the dynamics:
\[
    \mathbb{E}[R_{t+1} \mid S_t = s, A_t = a]
    = \sum_{r} r \sum_{s'} p(s', r \mid s, a)
    = \sum_{s'} \sum_{r} r \; p(s', r \mid s, a)
\]

Combining:
\[
    \boxed{\mathbb{E}_\pi[R_{t+1} \mid S_t = s]
    = \sum_{a} \pi(a \mid s) \sum_{s'} \sum_{r} r \; p(s', r \mid s, a)}
\]
