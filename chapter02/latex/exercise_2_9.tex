\section*{Problem}

Show that in the case of two actions, the soft-max distribution is the same
as that given by the logistic, or sigmoid, function often used in statistics
and artificial neural networks.

\section*{Solution}

\subsection*{Setup}

With $k = 2$ actions $a_1$ and $a_2$, the soft-max (equation 2.11) gives the probability of
selecting action $a_1$:
\[
    \Pr\{A_t = a_1\} = \pi_t(a_1)
    = \frac{e^{H_t(a_1)}}{e^{H_t(a_1)} + e^{H_t(a_2)}}
\]

\subsection*{Derivation}

Divide numerator and denominator by $e^{H_t(a_1)}$:
\[
    \pi_t(a_1) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{H_t(a_2) - H_t(a_1)}}
\]

Let $x = H_t(a_1) - H_t(a_2)$ (the preference difference). Then:
\[
    \boxed{\pi_t(a_1) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-x}} = \sigma(x)}
\]

This is exactly the \textbf{logistic sigmoid function} $\sigma(x)$.

Similarly:
\[
    \pi_t(a_2) = \frac{e^{H_t(a_2)}}{e^{H_t(a_1)} + e^{H_t(a_2)}}
    = \frac{1}{1 + e^{x}} = \sigma(-x) = 1 - \sigma(x)
\]

\subsection*{Interpretation}

The soft-max distribution over two actions is fully determined by the
\emph{difference} in preferences $x = H_t(a_1) - H_t(a_2)$, mapped through
the sigmoid:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $x \gg 0$: action $a_1$ is strongly preferred, $\sigma(x) \to 1$
    \item $x = 0$: no preference, $\sigma(0) = 0.5$ (equal probability)
    \item $x \ll 0$: action $a_2$ is strongly preferred, $\sigma(x) \to 0$
\end{itemize}

This is why logistic regression (binary classification) and two-action
soft-max are equivalent: both model a binary choice as a sigmoid of a
linear score difference. The soft-max generalizes the sigmoid to $k > 2$
choices.
